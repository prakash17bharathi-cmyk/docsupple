What is Ashwagandha?

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera), also known as Indian ginseng or winter cherry, is an adaptogenic herb used for thousands of years in Ayurvedic medicine. Adaptogens are plants believed to help the body adapt to stress and maintain balance. Ashwagandha contains active compounds called withanolides, which are the focus of modern scientific research.

Common Uses

  • Supporting stress response and relaxation
  • Promoting healthy sleep quality
  • Supporting mental clarity and cognitive function
  • Supporting mood and emotional well-being
  • Supporting physical performance and athletic recovery
  • Supporting energy levels and vitality
  • Supporting immune system function

Who May Benefit?

Ashwagandha supplementation may be beneficial for:

  • Individuals with high stress levels
  • People experiencing sleep difficulties
  • Those looking to support mental health and mood
  • People interested in cognitive support
  • Athletes looking for recovery support
  • Those experiencing occasional anxiety
  • Individuals interested in general wellness

Possible Side Effects

  • Digestive upset (nausea, diarrhea, constipation)
  • Headache
  • Drowsiness
  • May lower blood pressure and blood sugar
  • Potential interactions with sedative medications
  • Not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Not recommended for people with autoimmune conditions without medical guidance

Dosage

Typical supplemental dosages in studies:

  • 300-500 mg of standardized extract daily (usually containing 4-5% withanolides)
  • Divided into 1-2 doses
  • Often taken with water and meals
  • Effects typically manifest after 2-4 weeks of consistent use

Dosage varies by extract potency; follow label directions.

Forms Available

  • Ashwagandha root powder
  • Standardized extract capsules and tablets
  • Tinctures
  • Ashwagandha tea
  • Gummies

Active Compounds

Ashwagandha's therapeutic compounds include:

  • Withanolides (primary active alkaloids)
  • Alkaloids
  • Flavonoids
  • Tannins

Look for supplements standardized to contain 4-5% withanolides for consistent potency.

Important Notes

Ashwagandha may cause drowsiness, so take it in the evening or reduce dose if daytime somnolence occurs. Since ashwagandha may affect blood sugar and blood pressure, consult with a healthcare provider if you have diabetes or hypertension.
