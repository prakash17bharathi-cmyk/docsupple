Psyllium

← Back to Supplements

What is Psyllium?

Psyllium is a soluble fiber derived from the seeds of Plantago ovata, a plant native to India. It acts as a bulk-forming laxative and prebiotic fiber. When mixed with water, psyllium absorbs moisture and becomes viscous, forming a gel-like substance that supports digestive health and helps maintain regular bowel movements.

Common Uses

  • Supporting regular bowel movements and digestive regularity
  • Promoting overall digestive and gut health
  • Supporting heart health and cholesterol levels
  • Promoting satiety and healthy weight management
  • Supporting healthy blood sugar levels
  • Feeding beneficial gut bacteria (prebiotic effect)

Who May Benefit?

Psyllium supplementation may be beneficial for:

  • People with occasional constipation
  • Individuals with low dietary fiber intake
  • Those looking to support cholesterol levels
  • People with occasional digestive discomfort
  • Individuals managing blood sugar levels
  • Those looking for cardiovascular support

Possible Side Effects

  • Bloating and gas (especially initially)
  • Mild abdominal discomfort
  • Choking risk if not taken with adequate water
  • Rare allergic reactions
  • May interfere with medication absorption if not spaced properly

Dosage & Usage

Typical dosage: 1-2 teaspoons (3-6g) of psyllium husk powder, 1-3 times daily.

  • Mix thoroughly with 8 oz of water and drink immediately
  • Drink additional water afterward
  • Start with lower doses and increase gradually
  • Take 2 hours before or after medications
  • Best taken with meals

Forms Available

  • Psyllium husk powder
  • Psyllium fiber capsules
  • Pre-mixed psyllium drinks
  • Psyllium wafers

Dietary Sources

Soluble fiber is also found in:

  • Oats and oatmeal
  • Barley
  • Beans and legumes
  • Apples and pears
  • Citrus fruits
  • Flaxseeds

Important Notes

Always drink plenty of water when taking psyllium. Inadequate fluid intake can lead to choking or intestinal blockage. Not recommended for individuals with intestinal obstruction or bowel disorders without medical guidance.
