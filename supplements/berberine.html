Berberine

← Back to Supplements

What is Berberine?

Berberine is a naturally occurring alkaloid compound found in various plants including barberry, goldenseal, Oregon grape root, and phellodendron. Used for centuries in Ayurvedic and Traditional Chinese Medicine, berberine is increasingly studied for its potential effects on metabolic health and cellular function.

Common Uses

  • Supporting healthy glucose metabolism
  • Supporting cardiovascular health
  • Supporting weight management
  • Promoting healthy cholesterol levels
  • Supporting healthy inflammatory response
  • Supporting digestive health
  • Traditional use for digestive and antimicrobial support

Who May Benefit?

Berberine supplementation may be beneficial for:

  • Individuals with metabolic concerns
  • People looking to support blood sugar levels
  • Those interested in cardiovascular support
  • Individuals managing weight
  • People interested in natural metabolic support
  • Those with occasional digestive concerns

Possible Side Effects

  • Digestive upset (diarrhea, constipation, nausea)
  • Abdominal cramping or discomfort
  • Headache
  • Potential interactions with medications
  • May lower blood pressure
  • Not recommended during pregnancy

Dosage

Typical dosage in studies: 500mg 2-3 times daily with meals. Total daily intake typically ranges from 1,000-3,000mg.

  • Start with lower doses to assess tolerance
  • Take with meals to minimize digestive upset
  • Effects may take 2-4 weeks to manifest
  • Consult healthcare provider for personalized dosing

Sources

Berberine is extracted from plants including:

  • Barberry (Berberis vulgaris)
  • Goldenseal (Hydrastis canadensis)
  • Oregon grape (Mahonia aquifolium)
  • Phellodendron amurense
  • Coptis chinensis

Important Notes

Berberine may interact with numerous medications including diabetes medications, anticoagulants, and certain antibiotics. Consult with a healthcare provider before starting berberine, especially if you take medications or have existing health conditions.
