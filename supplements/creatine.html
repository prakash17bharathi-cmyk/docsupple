Creatine

← Back to Supplements

What is Creatine?

Creatine is a compound naturally produced in the body from amino acids (arginine, glycine, and methionine), primarily in the liver and kidneys. It is stored in muscles where it helps generate energy (ATP) during high-intensity exercise. Creatine supplementation increases muscle creatine stores beyond what diet alone provides.

Common Uses

  • Supporting muscle strength and power output
  • Enhancing athletic performance in high-intensity activities
  • Supporting muscle growth and mass gains
  • Improving muscle endurance
  • Supporting exercise recovery
  • Research suggests potential cognitive benefits

Who May Benefit?

Creatine supplementation may be beneficial for:

  • Athletes engaging in high-intensity, short-duration sports
  • Strength trainers and weightlifters
  • Individuals looking to maximize muscle gains
  • People engaged in intense intermittent exercise
  • Older adults (for muscle preservation)
  • Vegetarians (lower dietary creatine intake)

Possible Side Effects

  • Generally well-tolerated and safe
  • Water retention (slight increase in body weight)
  • Mild digestive upset (rare)
  • Dehydration risk if fluid intake is inadequate
  • Potential interactions with certain medications
  • Not recommended for individuals with kidney disease

Forms & Dosage

The most researched form is creatine monohydrate. Common protocols include:

  • Loading phase: 20g per day (5g, 4 times daily) for 5-7 days
  • Maintenance phase: 3-5g daily thereafter
  • Alternatively: 3-5g daily without loading phase (slower buildup)

Results typically appear after 1-2 weeks of consistent use.

Dietary Sources

Creatine is found naturally in:

  • Beef
  • Pork
  • Fish (especially herring and salmon)
  • Poultry

Vegetarians and vegans consume significantly less dietary creatine.

Important Notes

Ensure adequate hydration when supplementing with creatine. The supplement requires sufficient water intake to be absorbed and utilized effectively.
