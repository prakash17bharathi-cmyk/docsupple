What is Magnesium?

Magnesium is an essential mineral that plays a crucial role in over 300 biochemical reactions in the human body. It is required for muscle contraction, energy production, protein synthesis, and nervous system regulation. About 60% of the magnesium in your body is stored in bones, while the remainder is found in muscles, soft tissues, and body fluids.

Common Uses

  • Supporting muscle function and reducing cramping
  • Promoting relaxation and sleep quality
  • Supporting cardiovascular health
  • Maintaining bone strength
  • Supporting energy production
  • Reducing occasional muscle tension

Who May Benefit?

Magnesium supplementation may be beneficial for individuals who:

  • Have low dietary magnesium intake
  • Experience occasional muscle cramping or tension
  • Have difficulty sleeping
  • Are physically active or athletic
  • Experience high stress levels
  • Have certain digestive conditions affecting absorption

Possible Side Effects

  • Digestive upset (loose stools, diarrhea at high doses)
  • Nausea
  • Muscle weakness (at very high doses)
  • Interactions with certain medications

Forms & Dosage

Magnesium is available in various forms, including:

  • Magnesium Glycinate (gentle on digestion)
  • Magnesium Citrate (easily absorbed)
  • Magnesium Threonate (crosses blood-brain barrier)
  • Magnesium Malate (supports energy)
  • Magnesium Taurate (supports heart health)

Typical supplemental doses range from 150-400 mg daily, but consult with a healthcare provider for personalized dosing.

Dietary Sources

Magnesium is naturally found in:

  • Leafy green vegetables (spinach, kale)
  • Nuts and seeds (almonds, pumpkin seeds)
  • Whole grains
  • Legumes (beans, lentils)
  • Fish
  • Dark chocolate
