Turmeric

What is Turmeric?

Turmeric is a bright golden spice derived from the rhizome (root) of Curcuma longa, a plant native to Southeast Asia. Used for thousands of years in Ayurvedic and Traditional Chinese Medicine, turmeric contains the active compound curcumin, which is extensively studied for its potential health benefits. Turmeric provides the yellow color to curry powder and mustard.

Common Uses

  • Supporting healthy inflammatory response
  • Promoting antioxidant defense
  • Supporting joint health and mobility
  • Supporting cardiovascular health
  • Supporting brain health and cognitive function
  • Supporting digestive health
  • Supporting skin health

Who May Benefit?

Turmeric supplementation may be beneficial for:

  • Individuals with joint concerns or reduced mobility
  • People with inflammatory concerns
  • Those interested in antioxidant support
  • People interested in cardiovascular support
  • Individuals interested in brain health
  • Those interested in digestive support

Possible Side Effects

  • Digestive upset (nausea, diarrhea, stomach pain)
  • Headache
  • Skin rash (rare)
  • May increase bleeding risk at high doses
  • May affect blood sugar levels
  • May interact with medications, especially anticoagulants
  • Not recommended during pregnancy without medical guidance

Dosage & Bioavailability

Curcumin has low bioavailability. Typical dosages:

  • Turmeric powder: 500-2,000 mg daily
  • Curcumin extract: 150-500 mg daily
  • Enhanced-absorption formulas (with black pepper/piperine): Follow label directions

Black pepper (piperine) significantly increases curcumin absorption and is often included in supplements.

Forms Available

  • Whole turmeric powder (cooking spice)
  • Turmeric capsules and tablets
  • Curcumin extract supplements
  • Enhanced-absorption formulas (with bioenhancers)
  • Liposomal or nano-formulations

Culinary Use

Turmeric is used in cooking and curries. While culinary amounts are small, they still provide some benefits. Consuming turmeric with fats (olive oil, coconut oil) and black pepper enhances absorption.

Important Notes

Curcumin bioavailability is naturally low. Taking supplements with fat and black pepper (piperine) significantly improves absorption. If taking medications, consult with a healthcare provider about potential interactions.
