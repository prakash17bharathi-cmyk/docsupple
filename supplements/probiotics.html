Probiotics

← Back to Supplements

What are Probiotics?

Probiotics are live beneficial bacteria and yeasts that support the health and function of your gut microbiome. The human gut contains trillions of microorganisms that play crucial roles in digestion, immunity, and overall health. Probiotics help maintain balance in this microbial ecosystem.

Common Uses

  • Supporting digestive health and function
  • Promoting regular bowel movements
  • Supporting immune system function
  • Reducing occasional digestive discomfort
  • Supporting nutrient absorption
  • Promoting healthy intestinal barrier function
  • Supporting mental health (gut-brain axis)

Who May Benefit?

Probiotic supplementation may be beneficial for:

  • People with occasional digestive issues
  • Individuals taking or recovering from antibiotics
  • People with irritable bowel symptoms
  • Those with dietary imbalances
  • People with compromised immune function
  • Individuals with poor diet quality

Possible Side Effects

  • Generally well-tolerated
  • Mild digestive symptoms initially (bloating, gas)
  • Temporary increase in digestive activity
  • Rare: allergic reactions
  • Not recommended for severely immunocompromised individuals without medical guidance

Types & Strains

Common probiotic strains include:

  • Lactobacillus species (L. acidophilus, L. rhamnosus)
  • Bifidobacterium species (B. longum, B. breve)
  • Saccharomyces boulardii (beneficial yeast)

Different strains have different benefits; look for multi-strain formulas for broader support.

Dosage & CFU

Probiotic potency is measured in CFU (Colony Forming Units). Typical supplements contain 1-50 billion CFU per serving. Recommended doses vary by condition and strain.

Dietary Sources

Probiotics are naturally found in:

  • Yogurt (with active cultures)
  • Kefir
  • Sauerkraut
  • Miso
  • Tempeh
  • Kimchi
  • Other fermented foods

Storage & Quality

Probiotics are living organisms and require proper storage to maintain viability. Keep supplements in cool, dry conditions and check expiration dates.
